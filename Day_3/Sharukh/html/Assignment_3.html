1.Gajar Ka Halwa - Carrot Halwa Recipe

Important:

  • Preparation Time : 20 min
  • Cooking Time : 40 minutes
  • otal time : 1 hr
  • Serve For : 4

Ingredients:

  • Carrot or Gajar (3 cups) /grated
  • Milk (1 cup)
  • Clarified Butter or Ghee (2 tbsp)
  • Sugar (6 tbsp)
  • Cardamom (1/8 tsp) /crushed
  • Chopped Almonds (2 tbsp)
  • Cashew and Raisins (2 tbsp)

Direction:

  1. Peel and grate the carrots.
  2. In a heavy bottom pan, add ghee.
  3. Add grated carrots into the ghee and fry them for 10 minutes on medium heat by stirring constantly.
  4. Now add milk. Stir the mixture well, cover the pan with a lead and cook for 15 minutes.
  5. After 15 minutes, take off the lead. Stir well and add sugar.
  6. Combine the sugar evenly with the carrot mixture and cook on medium to high flame by stirring continuously.
  7. Meanwhile, add dry fruits and mix everything well.
  8. Stir and continue cooking until the halwa turns deep orange color.

Tips:

  • Serve your halwa hot, warm or even cold.
  • You can refrigerate this up to 7 days.





2.Spicy Red Chutney - Onion Tomato Chutney

Onion Tomato Chutney is a very quick and easy to make recipe. The basic ingredients of this Spicy Red Chutney is onion and tomato, but the tamarind and dry red chilli makes it simply irresistible as it provides hot and tangy touch into this recipe. If you are a chutney lover like me, try this lip smacking dip with idli or dosa. Or you can have it just like that.


Important:

  • Preparation Time : 5 minutes
  • Cooking Time : 15 minutes
  • Total Time : 20 minutes
  • Serve : 1 small bowl

Ingredients

  • Onion (2 medium size)
  • Tomato (1 big size)
  • Garlic (7-8 cloves)
  • Dry Red Chilli (3-4)
  • Tamarind Pulp (1/2 tsp)
  • Mustard Seeds (1/2 tsp)
  • Mustard Oil (1 tbsp)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Sugar (1/2 tsp)
  • Kashmiri Red Chilli Powder (1/2 tsp) /optional

Direction:

  1. Place onion, tomato, garlic cloves, dry red chilli and tamarind pulp into a blender and grind it into a smooth puree.
  2. Now heat mustard oil in a pan.
  3. When the oil is heated, add mustard seeds and wait for them to splutter.
  4. Add the ground puree into the pan, stir for a while.
  5. Into the mixture, add some salt, some sugar and kashmiri red chilli powder.
  6. Cook the chutney on low flame till oil separates from sides.
  7. Switch off the flame and transfer into a serving bowl.

Tips:

  • Serve with idli, dosa.
  • If you feel 3 red chillies are too much for you then reduce to 2.
  • The chutney becomes thick after sauteing. You can add little water to make it thinner consistency.
  • You can store this chutney up to 5 days in refrigerator.
